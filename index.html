Why this story matters

Vanilla is the world's second-most expensive spice. But behind its sweet aroma lies a fragile supply chain, reliant on one specific bee, one 12-year-old's hand-pollination method, and one climate-vulnerable island—Madagascar.

This interactive documentary uses climate data, trade routes, and price forecasts to trace how rising temperatures are rewriting the fate of an entire agricultural heritage. What happens when a crop's "perfect recipe" no longer exists anywhere on Earth?

📊 Data Sources

All data used in this interactive story comes from the following publicly available sources:

  • FAOSTAT — Global vanilla production volumes by country (1961–present).
  • World Bank / UN Comtrade — Import/export trade flows and commodity values.
  • CHELSA Bioclimate Projections — Future climate suitability scenarios (2050).
  • IndexBox / Tridge — Historical vanilla price indices and market volatility.
  • Duke University (PLOS Climate) — Farmer surveys on climate impacts in Madagascar.
  • Historical Archives — Colonial records and agricultural timelines (1520–present).

All datasets have been synthesized and normalized for narrative clarity.

ℹ️ About This Project

The Last Bite is an interactive scrollytelling dashboard created as a design assignment for SEG3125 — Design and Interactive Media at the University of Ottawa.

The goal was to apply Gestalt design principles (Proximity, Similarity, Figure-Ground, Continuity, and Common Fate) to transform complex climate and agricultural data into a compelling, narrative-driven user experience.

This project explores how data visualization can humanize abstract statistics—turning vanilla's 500-year journey into a lens for understanding climate vulnerability.
